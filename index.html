Reflection/WIL Placement Analysis

AKA Studio

07.2024 — 10.2024


Description

  1. During my WIL placement, I was extensively engaged in the development and management of a project for a restaurant system, concentrating largely on process automation and user interaction optimization. The project centered on two primary functionalities: drag-and-drop menu management and post automation, which constituted the backbone of the final product. Initially, I investigated low-code platforms such as AppSheet to optimize the development process by constructing a demonstration application. Nonetheless, the constrained capabilities of AppSheet prompted us to shift to more versatile tools, namely React and Node.js.


    I first configured MongoDB for data storage but subsequently changed to MySQL for the web application to better correspond with the project's requirements and enhance back-end operations. The choice to transition to MySQL was motivated by its enhanced interoperability with our system and the demands for organized data management. To enhance the system's automation capabilities, I utilized Zapier to automate Facebook posts, resolved API authorization issues, and examined the APIs of Facebook, Instagram, and Imgur for additional connection possibilities. Despite the initial promise of utilizing Imgur for image hosting within Zapier processes, its membership approach led us to dismiss this alternative.


    Alongside the technical work, I created a comprehensive user manual to assist end-users in properly navigating and utilizing the web platform. Despite my efforts and contributions, I acknowledged other situations where different activities would have yielded better outcomes.

Interpretation

  1. An aspect for improvement is the initial reliance on AppSheet. The transition from AppSheet, a no-code platform facilitating quick prototyping, to React and Node.js during the project led to inefficiencies. The time spent on AppSheet, although advantageous for understanding low-code solutions, would have been more effectively utilized by designing the project entirely in React from the beginning. This alteration would have provided enhanced flexibility and saved considerable development time.


    Furthermore, concurrently managing back-end development alongside front-end integration presented significant challenges. The learning curve related to these systems, particularly with API integration and automation, caused some delays. While I successfully employed Zapier for automation, I should have undertaken more comprehensive study into free or more versatile picture hosting alternatives earlier. The decision to explore Imgur first, which required a paid membership, led to complications that may have been avoided with a more thorough examination of free hosting options. Concerning team collaboration, although I communicated the technical information proficiently, I ought to have employed a more assertive approach in aligning team members with the project's long-term technical objective. This may have improved the overall efficiency of the transition between technologies, decreasing the time needed to adapt to new platforms.

Evaluation

  1. Upon review, the decision to invest in AppSheet, while advantageous for demo creation, ultimately represented a misjudgment that impeded the core development process. A more judicious approach would have been to favor React and Node.js from the beginning. This would have enabled enhanced flexibility, especially concerning scalability and the integration of customized features. In contrast, my expertise in addressing API-related issues and efficiently automating Facebook posts was a significant achievement. It demonstrated my inventiveness in overcoming technological challenges, particularly with external tools like Zapier. Configuring MongoDB and integrating it with MySQL demonstrated my strong back-end programming skills; nonetheless, balancing these tasks with front-end responsibilities was difficult. My inquiry into Instagram and Facebook APIs, while fruitful, might have been expanded earlier to identify more optimal options for image hosting. Furthermore, creating a user manual was a vital addition that highlighted my commitment to delivering a thorough product, so ensuring user satisfaction.

Plan

  1. I intend to enhance my capacity to assess and choose the most suitable tools for each phase of a project. Although AppSheet provided initial benefits, I saw the significance of matching tool selection with long-term project objectives from the beginning. In forthcoming projects, I will allocate additional time to investigate prospective platforms and technologies prior to making a commitment, so facilitating a more efficient workflow. To improve my API integration and automation capabilities, I plan to further investigate third-party platforms such as Zapier, while also examining alternate approaches that provide greater flexibility and cost-effectiveness. I will enhance my expertise in front-end frameworks like as React, enabling me to operate more fluidly in both front-end and back-end programming. This will allow me to address projects more comprehensively and minimize reliance on external platforms when feasible.


    I aim to improve my project management abilities by defining clearer technical objectives and aligning them with the team's competencies from the beginning. This will mitigate confusion during transitions and augment overall project efficiency. I will focus on full-stack development and API management, which are critical in modern web and application development. This placement has improved my understanding of technological tools, teamwork, and the need for flexibility in project development. It has reinforced my commitment to lifelong learning and the imperative of critically assessing my endeavours to improve future outcomes.

First Artefact: Drag-and-Drop Menu Function

Screenshot of the drag-and-drop Menu Function

  • Drag-and-Drop function

    Menu page

    • Context

    During my WIL placement, a principal responsibility assigned to me was the development of a drag-and-drop menu management system for a restaurant. The objective was to allow users to effortlessly rearrange menu items and personalize the daily menu via a visually accessible interface. Considering that contemporary web applications prioritize user-friendly interaction, it was essential to develop a seamless experience that necessitated no effort from the end user.


    Action

    I utilized React in conjunction with the react-dnd package to develop the drag-and-drop functionality. I initially investigated existing libraries and technologies that would facilitate the integration of drag-and-drop capabilities with minimal complexity. Upon evaluating several alternatives, I concluded that react-dnd provided the requisite flexibility and reactivity for this project. I organized the components to allow for the dynamic rearrangement of menu items, updating the database in real-time as modifications occurred. I also had to address difficulties such as maintaining state across sessions and ensuring that modifications were reliably reflected across various devices.


    Result

    The drag-and-drop feature was effectively included into the restaurant management system. Users could effortlessly drag and rearrange menu items without necessitating a technical comprehension of the system. This facilitated the restaurant staff's prompt updates to the daily menu. The client was notably delighted by the seamlessness of the interface and the flexibility it afforded in managing menu items.


    Learning

    This experience greatly enhanced my comprehension of front-end development, especially with the implementation of interactive elements. I improved my proficiency in React and acquired knowledge on optimizing state management for dynamic user interface updates. This is pertinent to my major in software development, where creating intuitive, user-centric interfaces is essential. This experience emphasized the significance of choosing appropriate tools, as my investigation into libraries such as react-dnd guaranteed that the drag-and-drop functionality was efficient and scalable for future applications.


    Second Artefact: Automating Facebook Posts with Zapier

    Screenshot of the Facebook Posts

  • Automating Facebook Posts

    Facebook page

    Context

    Another important part in the restaurant management system, automating the dissemination of daily menu changes on social media, specifically Facebook. The customer sought to minimize the manual effort involved in daily post uploads, necessitating an automated solution to optimize this activity and provide a consistent social media presence.


    Action

    A vital component of the project involved automating the daily menu updates on social media, specifically Facebook. Upon pressing the "Confirm Today's Special" button on the website, the menu data is transmitted to Zapier, initiating the posting process. This workflow activates the OpenAI API to generate a picture and a URL, which are subsequently included in the post and published on Facebook. This automation markedly diminished the manual labor demanded from the restaurant personnel, enhanced their social media participation, and guaranteed continuous updates without extra effort.


    Result

    The automation of Facebook posts, initiated by the "Confirm Today's Special" button, effectively optimized the restaurant's daily operations. The integration of Zapier and OpenAI enabled the system to autonomously manage both the content and graphic elements of each post, guaranteeing uniformity and a professional appearance on social media. This not only conserved considerable time and work for the restaurant staff daily, but it also ensured a continuous and engaging presence on Facebook. The client expressed significant satisfaction with the solution's reliability, as it facilitated the automatic posting of daily discounts without user input. Consequently, the restaurant's social media participation would be increased, allowing workers to concentrate on other essential responsibilities and improving overall efficiency.


    Learning

    This project enhanced my comprehension of automation and API integrations, particularly in the merging of several platforms to create a cohesive workflow. Through collaboration with Zapier and OpenAI, I acquired the skills to efficiently automate procedures encompassing data and media generation. It imparted the significance of choosing appropriate tools for particular jobs and enhancing processes to conserve time and augment efficiency. I acquired significant experience in resolving API issues, including picture production and URL linking. This experience is pertinent to my software development major, since it illustrates the application of automation in real-world contexts to enhance business operations. I will persist in investigating the capabilities of automation and AI integrations to improve system operation and user experience in upcoming projects.

    Evidences of ethical conduct and professional accountability

    1. During my WIL placement, I saw a scenario that underscored the significance of managing personal data with diligence. One of my primary responsibilities entailed the development of a login and registration mechanism for the restaurant management platform. This system gathered confidential user data, including names, email addresses, and passwords, throughout the registration process. Due to the sensitivity of this data, there were considerable ethical ramifications if it was not securely managed, particularly in light of the hazards linked to data breaches and illegal access.


      To mitigate these issues, we instituted encryption protocols to guarantee that all personal data, including passwords, was securely encrypted before to storage in the system's database. This indicated that, despite having access to the database, individuals would be unable to access the unprocessed personal information of users. Consequently, they would solely observe the encrypted data, thereby providing an extra layer of security. Furthermore, our encryption techniques adhered to best practices and regulatory standards, conforming to the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) that regulate the gathering and retention of personal data.


      Additionally, stringent access controls were implemented to guarantee that only authorized workers could access the requisite components of the system. In instances where team members required access to user data, they exclusively managed the encrypted versions, thereby enhancing the protection of users' personal information.


      Upon reflection, I contend that we may have enhanced our approach by doing frequent security audits and guaranteeing comprehensive staff proficiency in data privacy protocols. Providing training on data security and encryption techniques would have developed a more robust culture of accountability and ethical awareness among the team.


      In summary, this event highlighted how crucial it is to give professional responsibility and ethical behavior first priority while managing personal data. Through the encryption of sensitive data and the implementation of stringent security standards, we markedly diminished the risk of data exploitation. I will persist in implementing and advocating for robust ethical standards in my work, especially concerning data privacy and cybersecurity.
